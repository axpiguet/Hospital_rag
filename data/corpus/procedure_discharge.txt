TITLE: Standard Patient Discharge Procedure
DEPARTMENT: Nursing Administration
LAST REVIEWED: April 2026

1. DISCHARGE READINESS CRITERIA
A patient is considered ready for discharge when: vital signs are stable for 24 hours, pain is controlled with oral medication, the patient can tolerate oral intake, and any required mobility milestones (e.g. post-surgical ambulation) have been met.

2. DISCHARGE PLANNING
Discharge planning should begin within 24 hours of admission. The nursing team, attending physician, and (if applicable) social worker or physiotherapist coordinate to confirm home support, equipment needs, and follow-up appointments.

3. MEDICATION RECONCILIATION
Before discharge, a pharmacist or physician must reconcile the patient's home medication list against medications administered during the stay, and provide a printed updated medication list with dosing instructions.

4. DISCHARGE SUMMARY
The attending physician completes a discharge summary including: admission diagnosis, procedures performed, complications if any, discharge medications, and follow-up instructions. This must be completed within 24 hours of discharge and sent to the patient's primary care provider.

5. PATIENT EDUCATION
Nursing staff provide verbal and written instructions on wound care, activity restrictions, warning signs requiring return to hospital, and contact information for questions.

6. TRANSPORT
Patients without independent means of transport may request hospital-arranged transport through the patient services desk, subject to availability and clinical appropriateness.
